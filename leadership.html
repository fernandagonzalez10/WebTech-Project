I aim to create a community where team members can be encouraged and inspired to reach their full potential. Using empathy, communication and positivity, I'll create a work environment that is both fun and effective. Some lessons I have been able to learn through the art of cooking are:

1. Results are all that matter.

It's what the customer thinks of the product and service that counts. That's what creates repeat business and loyal customers. You may think you've come up with a brilliant dish, but if the folks don't like it, you failed.

2. It's not who you know but what you know.

Don't let anyone tell you success is about who you know. That's just an excuse for whiners who can't cut it. Great chefs know everything there is to know about making a restaurant business successful. Period.

3. Experience is overrated.

Even young chefs like me get better with time and with every dish that we make. It's about trial and error and about being passionate and perseverant.

4. Learn from failure and move on.

Failure is how we learn and grow. Failure teaches us how to do things differently. How to do things better. Great chefs don't dwell on their mistakes. They suck it up and do better next time. After all, there's always another meal.

5. Focus on core strengths.

Great chefs grow their business around their core strengths. There are lots of ways to diversify without going too far afield.

6. Smarts matter.

Nobody has ever been successful in the restaurant or cooking business by just doing the same stuff as everyone else. Sure, execution is critical, but innovation and creativity are also requirements for success. Like it or not, smarts matter.
